Digital Devices

Chapter 1: Digital Devices

1. Computers

Computers can be classified based on size, purpose, or architecture:

  • Size: Small laptops to large mainframe systems.
  • Purpose: Personal computers, servers, supercomputers.
  • Architecture: Design elements like memory organization or input/output systems.

Mainframe Computers

Large-scale data processing systems used by organizations.

  • Examples: Banking systems, weather forecasting.
  • Technology: High-performance CPUs, specialized hardware.
  • OS: IBM z/OS, Linux on zSystems.
  • Processing Power: Billions of instructions per second (BIPS).
  • Advantages: Reliability, security, high performance.
  • Disadvantages: Expensive, requires technical expertise.
  • Price: $500,000 to over $10 million.

Minicomputers

Used by small-to-medium-sized businesses for database management and ERP systems.

  • Examples: Research labs, manufacturing control.
  • OS: UNIX, Linux, VMS.
  • Processing Power: Millions of instructions per second (MIPS).
  • Advantages: Affordable, multi-user capability.
  • Disadvantages: Limited scalability, less common.
  • Price: $5,000 to $200,000.

Microcomputers

Compact, affordable computers for personal use.

  • Examples: Home computers, entry-level desktops.
  • OS: Windows, macOS, Linux.
  • Advantages: Affordable, easy to use.
  • Disadvantages: Limited power, not suitable for heavy multitasking.
  • Price: $300 to $2,000.

Desktop Computers

Personal computers designed to stay in one place.

  • Examples: Office workstations, gaming PCs.
  • OS: Windows, macOS, Linux.
  • Advantages: Customizable, upgradable.
  • Disadvantages: Stationary, requires peripherals.
  • Price: $500 to $3,000+.

Workstations

Specialized for high-performance tasks like video editing and CAD design.

  • Examples: Professional studios, research labs.
  • OS: Windows Professional, macOS, Linux distributions.
  • Advantages: Reliable, supports resource-intensive applications.
  • Disadvantages: Expensive, requires expertise.
  • Price: $2,000 to $10,000+.

2. Mobile Devices

Mobile Phones

Versatile devices for communication, entertainment, and productivity.

  • Applications: Social media, gaming, mobile banking.
  • Advantages: Multifunctional, portable.
  • Disadvantages: Fragile, short battery life.

Tablets

Portable touchscreen devices ideal for media consumption and light productivity.

  • Applications: Reading eBooks, online learning, video streaming.
  • Advantages: User-friendly, portable.
  • Disadvantages: Less powerful than laptops, fragile.
